# Voice & style — YouTubeAI blog

The blog's voice is plainspoken, opinionated, and grounded. Posts read like a thoughtful colleague writing notes for another thoughtful colleague — not like a content marketing template.

## Voice rules

- **Second-person + first-person plural.** "You" for the reader, "we" for the team. Never "I."
- **Active voice.** "We built this" not "this was built."
- **Short sentences when emphasizing, long sentences when explaining.** Vary cadence on purpose.
- **Concrete over abstract.** "A 3-hour podcast produces 30,000+ words of transcript" beats "podcasts are very long."
- **Name the tradeoff.** Every claim about a workflow being good should also name what it's bad for.
- **Don't oversell.** If the tool has limits in a specific use case, say so. Honesty is the differentiator vs. generic AI-content blogs.

## Banned phrases

These tag posts as low-effort generic content. Don't use them:

- "In today's fast-paced world…"
- "In the digital age…"
- "Let's dive in" / "Let's dive into"
- "Game-changer" / "revolutionary" / "cutting-edge"
- "Unlock the power of…"
- "Take your X to the next level"
- "The ultimate guide to…"
- "10 ways to…" (post format, not phrase — avoid listicle framings)
- "As an AI" — the post is signed `YouTubeAI Team`, not Claude.
- Stacked metaphors ("a Swiss Army knife on steroids that supercharges your productivity")

## Structural rules

- **No more than 3 H2 sections per ~1000 words.** Subheadings should partition real thought, not cosmetically break up text.
- **Lead each section with the conclusion.** First sentence states the point; rest of the paragraph supports it.
- **One idea per paragraph.** Multi-idea paragraphs are a sign of unfinished thinking.
- **Bullets only when the items don't have natural prose flow.** A 3-item bullet list with full sentences in each item should usually be prose.
